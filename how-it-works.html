The Process

From First Hello to Solar Power — Here's Every Step

Going solar with R&K Energized is simpler than most people expect. We handle the complexity so you don't have to. Here's exactly what happens from the moment we connect to the day your panels go live.

01

First Contact

We Start the Conversation

Every solar journey with R&K Energized begins with a real conversation — not a form submission or a chatbot. Our team reaches homeowners through door-to-door outreach, Facebook and Meta ads, and warm referrals. When we connect, we ask about your energy usage, your current bill, and your goals. No pitch. Just listening.

We review your most recent electric bills to understand your actual usage
We use your address and roof orientation to run a preliminary solar estimate
We explain every program option in plain English — Lease, PPA, Loan, or Cash
We answer every question you have before moving forward
🚪
02

Custom Design

We Engineer Your System

Once you decide to move forward, our team of solar engineers takes over the technical design. Using satellite imagery, your energy data, and local solar irradiance maps, we build a system sized specifically for your home — not a generic one-size-fits-all package.

Satellite roof analysis determines optimal panel placement and orientation
System is sized to offset your specific monthly energy usage
Shading, roof pitch, and local weather patterns are all factored in
Home battery storage is incorporated if selected
You review and sign a detailed proposal with all terms clearly outlined
📐
03

Permits & Approvals

We Handle All the Paperwork

This is usually the part that makes homeowners nervous — and it's the part you'll never have to touch. Our permit team manages every document, application, and approval on your behalf. You won't fill out a single form or make a single phone call to your utility company.

Local building permits applied for and obtained on your behalf
Utility interconnection agreements filed for you
HOA approval requests submitted if applicable
All electrical inspections coordinated and scheduled
Timeline: typically 4–8 weeks depending on local permit office speed
📋
04

Installation Day

Professional Install — Usually in One Day

When your permits are approved, we schedule your installation. A crew of licensed electricians and rooftop technicians shows up at your home and gets to work. Most residential solar installs are complete in a single day. When they leave, your system is mounted, wired, and ready for inspection.

Certified technicians complete the full installation
Panels mounted and wired to your home's electrical panel
Home battery installed if part of your plan
Your property is cleaned up completely before the crew leaves
R&K Energized checks in with you to make sure everything went smoothly
🔧
05

Inspection & Activation

Your System Goes Live

After installation, a local inspector signs off on the work — this is required by your city or county. Once that's done, your utility company authorizes the system to connect to the grid, and we flip the switch. From that moment, your home is powered by the sun.

City or county inspector approves the installation
Utility company authorizes Permission to Operate (PTO)
We remotely activate your system
You get app access to watch your production in real time
Your first solar-powered bill arrives — and you see the difference immediately
☀️
06

Ongoing Support

We Monitor. We Stay in Touch.

Your relationship with R&K Energized doesn't end at activation. We monitor your system 24/7 for performance issues and handle any repairs that come up — at no cost to you under the Lease or PPA programs. And we stay available as your local point of contact for questions, referrals, and anything else you need.

Our remote monitoring system watches your production daily
Any underperformance triggers an automatic service dispatch
All repairs and maintenance covered (Lease & PPA programs)
R&K Energized remains your local contact for questions and support
Refer neighbors and friends for ongoing community savings
🛡️

Common Questions

Things People Ask Us Every Day

Most single-family homes with a south-, east-, or west-facing roof and moderate to high electric bills are excellent candidates. We'll run a free analysis using your address and energy usage to tell you exactly what your system could produce and what you'd save — no commitment required.

You'll still receive a bill from your utility company, but it will be dramatically lower — often $0–$30 for basic grid connection fees. You'll also pay your monthly solar amount (for Lease or PPA), which is lower than what you were paying your utility before. The net result is a real, measurable monthly savings for most homeowners.

Solar actually makes your home more attractive to buyers — studies show solar homes sell faster and for more money. For Lease and PPA programs, the agreement can be transferred to the new owner (most buyers see it as a benefit) or, in some cases, paid off at closing. For Loan and Cash purchase, the system is part of the home's sale value.

Solar panels produce power whenever daylight hits them — even on overcast days (just at reduced capacity). At night, your home draws power from the grid as normal, or from a home battery if you've added one. Our systems are designed to produce enough power during daylight hours to offset your evening usage based on your local climate.
